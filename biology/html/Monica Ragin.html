Monica Y. Ragin, Ph.D., MBA

Assistant Professor

Office: Crain 212

Office Phone:  (864) 977-2083

Email:  monica.ragin@ngu.edu

Courses Taught: Embryology Laboratory, General Biology Laboratory, Human Biology Laboratory, Human Genetics, Integrated Biology Laboratory

Dr. Monica Ragin joined North Greenville University as an Assistant Professor in 2013. She earned her Bachelors of Science in Biology in 2006 at Presbyterian College. After which, she worked as a post-baccalaureate PREP Scholar, conducting complementary and alternative medicinal research (medical botany) and was trained in immunohistochemical laboratory techniques at Palmetto Richland Hospital (Columbia, SC). Dr. Ragin began graduate school as a Biomedical Sciences student in 2009 at the University of South Carolina School of Medicine and earned her PhD in Biological Sciences at the University of South Carolina in 2012. Her dissertation work is entitled "The Potential Role of Insulin (and Stress Response) Pathway Components in Breast Cancer Development and Progression."

Dr. Ragin's dissertation research was completed on breast cancer molecular and epidemiological genetics. While completing her PhD in Biological Sciences, Dr. Ragin simultaneously earned her MBA at the University of South Carolina.

She has published "Polymorphisms in Certain Diabetes Mellitus II - Related Genes May Play a Role in Disparities for Certain Risk Factors in Breast Cancer Amongst Blacks and Whites" in the Call and Response Journal 3(1): 47-56 and "Effect of Linear Furanocoumarins on Cell Migration in Breast Cancer and Genetic Analyses of African American and European American Breast Cancer Patients in South Carolina" in the African American Professors Program - 2012 Monograph Series. Dr. Ragin has also presented at several national conferences. She has also traveled to South Africa to serve as a Global Science and Engineering Fellow.

Prior to teaching, Dr. Ragin assisted with Media Marketing and Public Relations for the City of Columbia, SC; served as a Program Representative for Solicitor Dan Johnson's Office (Worthless Check Program); and worked in Congressman James Clyburn's office.

Dr. Ragin's research interests include determining the effectiveness of traditional medicinal compounds at inhibiting cell growth and potentially cancer progression and the molecular genetic methods of such compounds; and most recently, promoting awareness of the importance of folic acid intake to expecting mothers in unfortified nations.

Dr. Ragin enjoys being able to share the love of Christ with her students.
