Jan M. Foster, Ph.D.

Assistant Professor

Office: Crain 203

Office Phone:  (864) 663-0052

Email:  jfoster@ngu.edu

Courses Taught: Advanced Physiology, Anatomy and Physiology I and II, Histology, Human Biology

Dr. Jan M. Foster has a Bachelor’s of Science degree in Biology from Erskine College. Dr. Foster moved to Augusta, Georgia to earn her Ph.D. in Biomedical Sciences from the Medical College of Georgia in the field of protein regulation of renal and cardiovascular physiology. During this time, she attended Northside Baptist Church in Greenwood, SC where she felt a strong pull towards incorporating her faith into her understanding of human physiology. She decided to begin her teaching career in Christian higher education at North Greenville University. She joined the Biology Department in 2009 where she is teaching biology and health and wellness majors as well as courses for non-science majors.

Dr. Foster teaches primarily Anatomy and Physiology I and II, Human Biology, Histology, and Advanced Physiology. She has peer-reviewed publications and recently published a case study in physiology. She is active in the physiology professional community serving as a reviewer for two journals. She serves on numerous university committees and mentors students in undergraduate research projects using the soil nematode C. elegans. Dr. Foster is passionate about teaching all students to become health advocates for themselves and their families. She loves educating students about bodily function and infusing awe and admiration in how God has formed us.

Dr. Foster grew up overseas but her family settled in upstate SC and she considers it home. She is married and has two girls, ages 7 and 4. She enjoys spending time outdoors with her family and helping out with her daughters’ activities.
