What Will I Learn?

The Animal Science track of the Biology program provides students with a comprehensive, Christian-based education in equestrian science. Our Animal Science program offers two tracks in equestrian studies. Students in both tracks will earn a Bachelor of Science in Biology with a focus on equine science.

The Equestrian Track

The equestrian track provides students with courses focused on the study of horses. Graduates of this track will be trained in horse care and maintenance, ridership, and equine biology. Career opportunities in this track include ranch management, private training, breeding, and organizing equestrian events.

The Pre-Professional Track

The pre-professional track of the Animal Science program is designed for students who plan to pursue graduate work in equine science or veterinary medicine. The pre-professional track provides a strong foundation in science, including chemistry, advanced equine biology, horse health, and nutrition. Graduates of the pre-professional track can continue their studies to become a veterinary technician, a veterinarian, or pursue opportunities in graduate research and teaching.

Our Facilities

Construction is underway for a barn and related buildings on the 48-acre equestrian campus with views of the Blue Ridge Mountains. The facility will include everything necessary to maintain horses including 30 stalls, a tack room, a wash room, classrooms and faculty offices.
